REUTERS LAUNCHES MEXICAN MONEY MARKET DEALING SERVICE

NEW YORK, May 30, 1995 -- Reuters has launched a customized, real-time dealing service designed to improve the efficiency of trading in Mexican money market instruments by providing traders access to market news, information, and execution capabilities on a single, fully integrated system. The service is currently in use by 15 major banking institutions in Mexico.

Reuters Mexican Money Market Dealing service enables market participants to trade all major Mexican money market instruments -- including cetes, pagares and tesobonos -- in Spanish under local market trading conventions and rules. The forms-based dealing application allows traders to conduct up to eight simultaneous dealing conversations as well as to contact multiple counterparties in under three seconds. Prices contributed by participants, best bid and offer data, and prices of trades made on the system are automatically captured in the Reuter information database, providing dealers with an excellent source of real-time pricing information.

"The Reuter Mexican Money Market Dealing service takes a user-friendly approach to helping traders execute an ever-increasing volume of trades with a greater degree of price certainty, a key objective in today's volatile marketplace," said Tacio Carvalho, Senior Vice President Latin America, Reuters America.

"This customized dealing application utilizes our existing operational infrastructure and dealing network. The service is the first in a series of new dealing applications being considered by Reuters to address the needs of various local and emerging financial markets around the globe," said Rosalyn Wilton, Managing Director Transaction Products, Reuters Holdings PLC.

Reuters America is the chief operating division in the Americas of Reuters Holdings PLC (NASDAQ: RTRSY). Reuters supplies the global business community and news media with a wide range of products, including real-time financial data, transaction systems, access to numerical and textual historical databases, news, graphics, still photos and news video. Information is obtained from around 220 exchanges and over-the-counter markets, from approximately 4,130 subscribers who contribute data directly to Reuters, and from a network of about 1,640 journalists, photographers and cameramen. Some 300,000 clients access Reuter information, some using their own terminals to do so.
