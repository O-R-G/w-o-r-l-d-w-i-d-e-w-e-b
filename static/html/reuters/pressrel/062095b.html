REUTERS SIGNS AGREEMENT WITH PROVIDER OF TRANSCRIPT SERVICES

NEW YORK, July 20, 1995 -- Reuters America and Federal Document Clearing House, Inc.(FDCH) have entered into an agreement to provide Reuters subscribers with electronic transcripts of government and industry events.

Founded in 1993, FDCH is a leading provider of government- and industry- related information products and a pioneer in making primary source government material available to on-line computer users. The FDCH Transcript Service provides complete, fully indexed and text searchable coverage of news conferences and press briefings, informal Presidential statements, State, Justice and Defense Department briefings, and full gavel-to-gavel coverage of key Congressional hearings.

Under the new agreement, current subscribers to the Reuter Transcript Report have been transitioned to the FDCH Transcript Service. Transcripts provided by FDCH will be distributed to Reuter Transcript Report subscribers with no programming changes.

Reuters America Inc. is the chief operating entity in the Americas of Reuters Holdings PLC (NASDAQ:RTRSY). Reuters supplies the global business community and media with a wide range of products, including real-time financial data, transaction systems, access to numerical and textual historical databases, news, graphics, still photos and news video. Information is obtained from around 220 exchanges and over-the-counter markets, from approximately 4,130 subscribers who contribute data directly to Reuters, and from a network of about 1,640 journalists, photographers and cameramen. Some 300,000 clients access Reuter information, some using their own terminals to do so.
