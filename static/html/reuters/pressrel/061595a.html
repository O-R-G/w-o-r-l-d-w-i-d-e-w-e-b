REUTERS ANNOUNCES ACCESS TO INFORMATION ON KEY MEXICAN ECONOMIC INDICATORS

NEW YORK, June 5, 1995 -- Reuters announced that it has begun carrying comprehensive information on key Mexican financial and economic indicators provided directly by the Mexican Finance Ministry. Sources contributing data include the Mexican Central Bank and the National Institute of Statistics, Geography and Informatics. The data, which is updated on a regular basis, includes such information as trade balance, current accounts, inflation rates and interest rates to provide a complete picture of the dynamics of the Mexican economy.

"This is an unprecedented and ambitious financial disclosure by the Mexican government," said Tacio Carvalho, Senior Vice President Latin America, Reuters America. "Timing is critical in investment, especially where volatile emerging markets are concerned, and this provides a timely source of much needed information on the Mexican economy. Reuters is privileged to be the first carrier of this information internationally."

Providing this information demonstrates a recognition by the Mexican Finance Ministry that market transparency will be a key factor in Mexico's economic recovery. Commenting on the economic openness, Aliza Chelminsky, Advisor to Mexican Finance Minister Guillermo Ortiz, said, "We are responding to the informational needs expressed by the markets. This is the first step in what will be a dynamic process to provide valuable information to the business community and to the people who move the markets."

Reuters America is the chief operating entity in the Americas of Reuters Holdings PLC (NASDAQ:RTRSY). Reuters supplies the global business community and media with a wide range of products, including real-time financial data, transaction systems, access to numerical and textual historical databases, news, graphics, video. Information is obtained from around 220 exchanges and over-the-counter markets, from approximately 4,130 subscribers who contribute data directly to Reuters, and from a network of about 1,640 journalists, photographers and cameramen. Some 300,000 clients access Reuter information, some using their own terminals to do so.
