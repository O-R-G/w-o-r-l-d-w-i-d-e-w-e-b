NBC SIGNS LONG-TERM, GLOBAL AGREEMENT WITH REUTERS

NEW YORK, March 8, 1995 -- NBC and Reuters have entered into a comprehensive new agreement that allows NBC worldwide use of Reuters news, text wires and financial data services in its programming.

The 10-year agreement, which is effective immediately, applies to NBC and all of its existing and future services worldwide, including NBC News, CNBC, CNBC Asia, NBC Super Channel, NBC News Channel and Canal de Noticias NBC. Each NBC service will be able to use on a global basis the Reuters information services to which it subscribes.

Reuters has supplied news and financial information to NBC previously. This new agreement builds on the companies' traditional relationship to create a model for NBC's future use of Reuters information worldwide.

"This exciting development more closely aligns CNBC, the world's leader in business television, with Reuters, the leader in gathering financial news, data and information," said Roger Ailes, President, CNBC and America's Talking. "Given our recent successful expansion into Europe and Asia, we are delighted to have the Reuters resources for all of our programming services. CNBC clearly is on its way to becoming the essential televised source of business information for today's investors around the globe."

Mark Wood, Editor-in-Chief and an executive director of Reuters, said: "This new agreement helps to reinforce our long-standing partnership with NBC and take it well into the next century. We are pleased to be able to contribute to the expansion of NBC's television activities and to open up new areas of cooperation between our organizations."

Stated Andrew Lack, President of NBC News: "For years, NBC News has enjoyed a productive relationship with Reuters and its subsidiary, Reuters Television, with whom we exchange news video. We couldn't be happier that the long-term partnership between NBC and Reuters continues to prove more successful and provides us with a solid base to seek new opportunities together."
