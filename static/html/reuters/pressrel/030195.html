REUTERS TELEVISION APPOINTS MANAGING DIRECTOR

New York, NY, March 1, 1995- Reuters (NASDAQ:RTRSY) announced today it has appointed David Kogan as Managing Director of Reuters Television Limited.

Kogan, 37, will direct the commercial development and expansion of Reuters television services internationally. He also retains his present responsibilities as Director of Media Services in the UK and Ireland (UKI).

Kogan joined Visnews as Managing Editor in 1988 and in 1992 was named Deputy Head of News, Visnews, and Managing Editor of GMTV, the leading UK breakfast television program. Visnews became a wholly-owned subsidiary of Reuters in late 1992 and was renamed Reuters Television. Kogan was appointed Manager, Television and Radio, Reuters UKI in 1993 and Director, Media Services, UKI in 1994.

Kogan succeeds Enrique Jara who has been named Director of Television and New Media Development, Latin America, based in Argentina. Jara continues to serve as Chairman of TeleNoticias, the 24-hour Spanish-language television news network launched in Latin America and Spain in December by Reuters and three partners.

Jara will focus on the development of TeleNoticias and also develop markets in Latin America for both Reuters Television and Reuters NewMedia Inc., which is building multimedia opportunities for the company.

Jara, 55, joined Reuters in 1970 as a correspondent and later held a variety of managerial posts in Latin America and Europe before being appointed Managing Director, Latin America and the Caribbean, based in Buenos Aires, in 1983. He was named Director of Media Products, Reuters, and Managing Director, Reuters Television, in 1993.

Reuters Television Limited is a leading international television news agency. With 70 bureaus serving 650 broadcasters in over 80 countries, Reuters Television coverage is estimated to reach some half-billion households worldwide. Content for the television services is drawn from Reuters entire reporting network of over 1,600 journalists, TV crews and photographers in 128 bureaus.
