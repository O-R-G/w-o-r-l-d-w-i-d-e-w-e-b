REUTERS LAUNCHES FINANCIAL TV SERVICE IN THE US

New York, May 24, 1995 - Reuters (NASDAQ: RTRSY) today announced the U.S. launch of Reuters Financial Television (RFTV), its television service for the foreign exchange, debt and treasury markets. This follows its introduction in Europe last year.

RFTV delivers live coverage of key market events, providing a powerful tool for making split-second trading decisions. It presents daily briefings and then goes live intermittently for important economic and financial events including speeches, summit meetings, key trade statistics and exclusive interviews with leading economic figures and financial analysts.

Dedicated cameras have been installed in more than 40 trading rooms in the UK, Continental Europe, Asia and the U.S. to gather live commentary from analysts as events occur.

Paul Eedle, Editor and Senior Vice President, Reuters America, said, "We are delighted to make Reuters Financial Television available in the U.S. RFTV is an important new trading tool that will help our customers trade more efficiently because they will get news faster than by any other means. You can't get faster than live."

RFTV is a global service produced by specialist teams of financial journalists and television professionals drawing on information from Reuters 128 news bureaus, 70 of which can currently provide TV coverage. It is transmitted digitally and is generally viewed in a window on the user's computer terminal alongside existing data signals which show textual news and market prices from Reuters.

Donald Nordberg, Vice President and Financial Editor, Reuters America, said, "RFTV focuses on macroeconomic news and analysis, making it especially useful to participants in the foreign exchange and debt markets."

The service is currently available to users in 18 countries in Europe and North America. It will also be launched in Japan during the second half of the year.

Reuters America is the chief operating division in the Americas of Reuters Holdings PLC, the international news and financial information services group.
