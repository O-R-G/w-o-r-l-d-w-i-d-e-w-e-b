REUTERS BRIGHTSTAR CUTS RATES, CITING STREAMLINING AND INCREASED EFFICIENCY

New York, June 19, 1995 -- Reuters BrightStar Communications, a leading provider of international satellite broadcasting, announced a plan to lower rates and pass on cost savings, gained through streamlining and increased efficiency, to its customers. The rate reductions went into effect on May 1.

"BrightStar is very aware of the pressures on our customers to cut their costs," said Paul Eedle, Editor and Senior Vice President, Reuters America Inc. "In order to continue to offer the best combination of quality service at competitive rates, we took a hard look at our services, streamlining where possible, and are now operating more efficiently than ever. As a result, we are offering a new 10 Minute Rate, Reduced Hourly Rates and new Digital Rates. These new rates benefit BrightStar's Satellite Service customers in Europe, North America and Latin America."

The new 10 Minute Rate was created with BrightStar's news and sports daily customers in mind. BrightStar's Reduced Hourly Rates now include both space and ground segments in trans-Atlantic transmissions and the new Digital Rates provide 8.5 megabit service between BrightStar's hub and facilities in London and Washington, D.C. IRD's (Integrated Receiver Decoders) will be rolled out to customers in Latin America and Europe in the next two months to allow access to the digital channels.

Reuters BrightStar, one of the leading providers of international satellite transmission services in the world, provides regular and special event transmissions and has built a wide range of satellite management services for its customers. BrightStar provides cost and quality-effective services via its state-of-the-art international Broadcast Centers located in London, Moscow and Washington, D.C.

Reuters America Inc. is the chief operating division in the Americas of Reuters Holdings PLC (NASDAQ:RTRSY), the news and financial information services group.
