JAMAICAN STUDENT WINS FIRST REUTER FOUNDATION AWARD AT UNIVERSITY OF FLORIDA

Gainesville, Florida, March 23, 1995-- A journalism student from Jamaica, Tasha Rodney, has won the first scholarship from a US $150,000 endowment fund set up by the Reuter Foundation at the University of Florida.

Miss Rodney, 20, who is majoring in journalism reporting, received a grant of US $1,000 for her final year at the University's College of Journalism and Communications.

The grant is the first payment from the fund to which the Reuter Foundation is contributing $100,000 over five years, matched by a grant of $50,000 from the State of Florida.

The fund offers scholarships to minority and foreign students in journalism, particularly from Latin America and the Caribbean, at the University of Florida, which has one of the largest undergraduate journalism programs in the United States.

The Reuter Foundation is an educational trust established in 1982 by Reuters, the international news organization, to promote high standards in international journalism through study and training. As the charitable arm of Reuters Holdings PLC, it also supports a wide range of humanitarian causes.
