ABC RADIO AND REUTERS EXPAND ABC NEWSWIRE

New York, June 26, 1995 -- ABC Radio Networks and Reuters America Inc have entered into a new five-year agreement to expand ABC NewsWire, the 24-hour news service. The agreement is designed to enhance the depth and quality of the information fed to affiliate radio stations. After doubling its customer base in the last year, ABC will now target several thousand more radio stations in need of a comprehensive broadcast wire service.

Effective July 3, 1995, Reuters will combine coverage from States News Service with Reuters local business and financial news reported from its 21 U.S. bureaus. States News Service is a leading independent provider of state-specific news including relevant federal legislative and regulatory developments on a state-by-state basis. Reuters, one of the most respected names in worldwide news coverage, already provides ABC NewsWire subscribers with national and international news stories.

With the expanded service, ABC NewsWire subscribers will now be equipped with a complete, affordable news wire delivered in a concise, broadcast-ready presentation.

Richard Lorenzo, General Manger, ABC NewsWire, said "With these enhancements from Reuters and States News Service, ABC NewsWire is providing stations with tools that are affordable and needed to expand their news product and give them a greater edge over the competition."

Designed for individual station needs, ABC NewsWire is menu-driven and satellite-delivered using state-of-the-art technology. It allows radio stations the opportunity to customize their news and information programming in an efficient and affordable manner. Radio stations are provided with crisis coverage, bulletins and breaking news from around the world, including hourly news summaries, sports summaries and running scores, business news reports and stock market updates, entertainment reports, morning show prep material, local weather reports, advisories and instant warnings.

"We believe the alliance of ABC, Reuters and States News Service makes ABC NewsWire the best value radio stations across the country can buy. Everything's included -- local, national, international and business news, as well as sports and weather," said Paul Eedle, Executive Vice President and Editor America, Reuters America Inc.

Reuters America Inc. is the chief operating division in the Americas of Reuters Holdings PLC (NASDAQ: RTRSY). Reuters supplies the global business community and news media with a wide range of products, including real-time financial data, transaction systems, access to numerical and textual historical databases, news, graphics, still photos and news video. Information is obtained from around 220 exchanges and over-the-counter markets, from approximately 4,130 subscribers who contribute data directly to Reuters, and from a network of about 1,640 journalists, photographers and cameramen. Some 300,000 clients access Reuter information, some using their own terminals to do so.

ABC Radio Networks serves more than 3,400 radio stations and is the largest radio network in the United States with an estimated reach of over 110 million persons per week. ABC Radio broadcasts six full-service line networks, Paul Harvey News & Comment, the ESPN Radio Network, long-form programming, ABC 24-hour formats, ABC News, sports and daily and weekly features. ABC Radio Networks is a subsidiary of Capital Cities/ABC, Inc. and is heard in over 90 countries.
