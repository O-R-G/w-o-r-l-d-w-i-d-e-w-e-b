REUTERS ADDS U.S. CONTENT TO BUSINESS INFORMATION DATABASE

NEW YORK, NY, February 1, 1995--Reuters NewMedia Inc. has secured international and domestic rights from Information Access Company (IAC) to distribute content from PROMT and Computer Database. These databases offer extensive coverage of international and U.S. industry journals, newsletters, business magazines, and daily newspapers.

Reuters will distribute this content, which includes full-text references and abstracts from over 1,200 publications, as part of the Reuter Business Information products database which it markets to corporations. The arrangement will triple the number of titles in the Reuter database.

PROMT carries some 1,100 titles including full text of Adweek, Media Week, and Hollywood Reporter, and abstracts of Business Week, INC., Newsweek, USA Today, Chicago Sun Times, Washington Post, Newsday, and San Francisco Chronicle.

Computer Database includes more than 100 leading computer, electronics and telecommunications sources that focus on companies, products and technologies. Among these are Byte, Communications Week, Computerworld, PC World, PC Week, and Windows Magazine.

According to Alexa Smith, Business Information Products Manager, Reuters NewMedia: "The size and breadth of IAC's PROMT and Computer Database augments the existing strength of Reuters Business Information databases with a substantial number of new titles, and particularly new U.S. content. These IAC databases are extremely well known and have been requested by Reuter customers world wide."

IAC is a leading supplier of periodical reference products and database services. Its holdings include the well known Predicasts databases the largest of which, Predicasts Overview of Markets & Technology (PROMT), is a widely used database of international and U.S. titles covering companies, products, markets and emerging technologies.

Reuters developed its Business Information products to enable corporations to gather business intelligence from a vast array of sources. These products provide ready access to a database containing business information on thousands of publicly-traded and unlisted companies dating back five years. There are more than 600 international sources including newspapers, magazines, trade and professional journals, databases and news wires, including Reuters.

Later this year, when the IAC databases become available to Reuter customers, the list of international sources will have surpassed 1,800.

In the U.S., Reuters America markets these products to the financial services industry and the financial functions within corporations. Reuters NewMedia markets to all other functions within corporations.

Reuters NewMedia Inc. is a U.S.-based company formed by Reuters Holdings PLC (NASDAQ: RTRSY) in 1994 to develop multi-media information and transaction services for emerging corporate and consumer markets. Reuters America Inc. is the chief operating entity of Reuters Holdings in the Americas.

IAC has contractual agreements with some 1,400 publishers for the distribution of full-text, and has its own proprietary data in the form of value-added indexing and original abstracts. The IAC databases contain references from over 6,250 publications offering an extensive range of business, marketing, management, legal, healthcare, computer and general interest information. Information Access Company is owned by The Thomson Corporation (Toronto: TOC) and is part of The Thomson Business Information unit.
