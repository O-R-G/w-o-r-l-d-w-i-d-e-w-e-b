REUTERS INCREASES ITS SHARE IN ADVALUE TO 50 PERCENT

NEW YORK, May 30, 1995 -- Reuters has increased its equity shareholding in AdValue Media Technologies, Inc. to 50 percent through the purchase of shares held by minority investors Radnor Venture Partners and Cambridge Technology Partners.

Reuters paid an aggregate of $3.75 million to acquire the 19 percent stake held by Radnor and Cambridge, and to make an additional equity investment in the company. Buford Smith, President, Reuters NewMedia Inc., said: "We are pleased to have increased our position in AdValue to 50 percent. The investment reflects our satisfaction with AdValue's progress since our relationship with the company began in late 1993."

AdValue Media Technologies markets computerized advertisement-tracking and communications services to advertising agencies, advertising sales representatives, and TV stations.

AdValue's clients include advertising agencies such as Bates Advertising, Grey Advertising, McCann-Erickson, Young & Rubicam, NW Ayer and Ogilvy & Mather, media-buying specialist firms such as Zenith and Media Buying Service, and the TV advertising representative firm Petry, Inc. Its television clients include the NBC and Post-Newsweek station groups as well as the Group W Television stations of Westinghouse Broadcasting Co. AdValue maintains formal joint-marketing relationships with the media ratings analysis providers Tapscan, Inc. and BMP.

AdValue Media Technologies, Inc. is a 50/50 joint venture of Reuters, managed in the U.S. by Reuters NewMedia Inc., a subsidiary of Reuters Holdings PLC (NASDAQ: RTRSY), and Westinghouse Broadcasting Company, a division of Westinghouse Electric Corporation (NYSE: WX).
