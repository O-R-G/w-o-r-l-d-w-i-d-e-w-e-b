SKY NEWS AND REUTERS JOIN FORCES

New York, NY, February 2, 1995--British Sky Broadcasting and Reuters announced today they have formed an alliance to build a major new force in international television news.

Under the agreement Sky News will retain editorial control of its output while Reuters will supply and manage UK and global news gathering for the 24-hour Sky News channel. Reuters will also produce several programs tailor-made for Sky News.

Sam Chisholm, Chief Executive of British Sky Broadcasting, said: "Sky News is recognized internationally for the high quality of its news and current affairs programming. This strength, coupled with Reuters enormous international infrastructure and facilities, will provide a platform for the global expansion of Sky News."

Mark Wood, Executive Director, Reuters, said: "We are excited by this opportunity to expand a relationship with Sky News which dates back to its launch. This agreement will lead to a significant increase in the news-gathering resources we now commit to Sky News and provides a new opportunity for us to develop our programme-making expertise."

Sky News has been broadcasting for five years and now can be seen by an estimated 70 million people in close to four million homes in the UK and 19 million homes in 40 countries throughout Europe. In that time, Sky has received many of the highest industry awards, including The Royal Television Society News Award for coverage of the Bishopsgate bombing and Ostend tanker fire. Most recently, a Gold Medal for News Reporter of the Year was awarded to Sky's South Africa correspondent, Jeremy Thompson.

Reuters Holdings PLC (NASDAQ:RTRSY) operates the largest international television news agency with over 70 bureaus worldwide providing 24-hour international coverage for 650 broadcasters in over 80 countries. Reuters also produces television programming for broadcast and financial markets worldwide.
